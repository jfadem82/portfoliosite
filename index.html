What's Good?

A long time ago,  in a galaxy far,  far from Silicon Beach,  I was born at St. John's Hospital in Santa Monica,  and raised in Venice,  with deep roots in the LA punk rock and hip hop cultures.  My dad bought a Mac SE in the late 80's, and i used it to play a lot of Dark Castle.  I guess you could say that computers have been as much of a constant in my life as almost anything else.

My childhood dream was  (Surprise!)  to work in the music business,  a goal which I accomplished in 2007,  when I got my first "Official Gig" as an audio engineer.  Between then and now,  I've been blessed to work with artists whose posters I had on my wall,  as well as the privilege of calling some of them my friends.  I've toured the US,  and have Canadian,  British,  Italian,  and Mexican stamps in my passport.  I'd say 15 year-old me is pretty stoked.

Last year,  I became a partner at Wolf's Head,  a premium men's clothing store and barbershop located in Santa Barbara that I'm still involved with,   and very passionate about. I'm lucky to have 2 amazing business partners and close friends.

It was while teaching myself to do basic SEO for our site that I first considered coding as a career that I could excel in.  I mean,  I was already used to sitting for 12 or more hours in front of a computer screen while mixing for the likes of  DJ Muggs,  co-producing one of   The Source Magazine's "Top 50 Hip-Hop Songs of 2010",  and engineering"Pi√±ata",  which came in at number six on  Rolling Stone's "40 Best Rap Albums of 2016".  All of that was accomplished via knowledge I gained from a trade school,  so I didn't see why the same couldn't be true for venturing into a tech career of some sort. 

After much internal debate and careful consideration,  I chose to attend General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program in Santa Monica.  Over the course of 12 weeks,  I received over 420 hours of instruction,  and at least as many working independently to build a strong foundation in JavaScript,  Node.js,  MongoDB,  Angular.js,  SQL Databases,  and Ruby on Rails.  I've exceeded my own expectations of what I could accomplish in 12 weeks.  I led the only group to receive a perfect score on a project,  and won an attendance award (Which,  if I were to tell you of my previous school endeavors outside of Musicians Institute,  you would find as hilarious as I do).  I never thought I'd be able to say with confidence that I'm a capable Web Developer in such a short time.  But,  then,  I never thought I'd be able to say I worked on "Detox".
                                       And here we are.

    ...I look forward to meeting and working with you.

Calisthenics

Calisthenics is disrupting the world of fitness by enabling extremely scalable workouts using nothing but your bodyweight (yes that means free) to develop high levels of strength, balance, flexibility, endurance, and body awareness.

The human body was designed to move itself. Calisthenics focuses on a holistic approach to physical capacities of the human body, rather than aesthetics. Regardless, the movements are more comprehensive than typical weightlifting, and lead toward a more natural, proportional physique.

Calisthenics is a unique combination of physical challenge, mental determination, and socialization, which I believe will make a profound difference in the fitness industry. The movement is young and the identity of calisthenics in America is still forming, which means massive progress is left to be made. There are already companies in Europe that design and install workout parks intended for free public use. This could be huge in the US, just look at the popularity of muscle beach in Santa Monica.

Imagine that, free gyms in cities all over the country. A stark difference from today's world, but the future is at hand.. Check out my calisthenics experiences on
