What's Good?

A long time ago, in a galaxy far, far from Silicon Beach, I was born at St. John's Hospital in Santa Monica, and raised in Venice. My roots lie primarily in the LA punk rock and hip hop cultures. My dad bought a Mac SE in the late 80's, and i used it to play a lot of Dark Castle. I guess you could say that computers have been as much of a constant in my life as almost anything else.

My childhood dream was (Surprise!) to work in the music business, a goal which I accomplished in 2007, when I got my first "Official Gig" as an audio engineer. Between then and now, I've been blessed to work with artists whose posters I had on my wall, as well as the privilege of calling some of them my friends. I've toured the US, and have Canadian, British, Italian, and Mexican stamps in my passport. I'd say 15 year-old me is pretty stoked.

Last year, I became a partner at Wolf's Head, a premium men's clothing store and barbershop located in Santa Barbara that I'm still involved with, and very passionate about. I'm lucky to have 2 amazing business partners and close friends.

It was while teaching myself to do basic SEO for our site that I first considered coding as a career that I could excel in. I mean, I was already used to sitting for 12 or more hours in front of a computer screen while mixing for the likes of DJ Muggs, co-producing one of The Source Magazine's "Top 50 Hip-Hop Songs of 2010", and engineering"Pi√±ata", which came in at number six on Rolling Stone's "40 Best Rap Albums of 2014". All of that was accomplished via knowledge I gained from a trade school, so I didn't see why the same couldn't be true for venturing into a tech career of some sort.

After much internal debate and careful consideration, I chose to attend General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program in Santa Monica. Over the course of 12 weeks, I received over 420 hours of instruction, and at least as many working independently to build a strong foundation in JavaScript, Node.js, MongoDB,
Angular.js, SQL Databases, and Ruby on Rails.
I've exceeded my own expectations of what I could accomplish in 12 weeks. I led the only group to receive a perfect score on a project, and won an attendance award (Which, if I were to tell you of my previous school endeavors outside of Musicians Institute, you would find as hilarious as I do). I never thought I'd be able to say with confidence that I'm a capable Web Developer in such a short time. But, then, I never thought I'd be able to say I worked on "Detox".

And here we are.

...I look forward to meeting and working with you.

Portfolio

Degenerate Blackjack

An object-oriented JavaScript version of the classic card game. The card images are being fetched from a remote server. Other Technologies used: HTML & CSS
•Project completed in a 5 day sprint.

Wolf's Head Coffee

A full stack Ruby on Rails web app that mocks up a coffee subscription site my Wolf's Head partners and I are currently developing, complete with a functioning blog. Other Technologies used: HTML, CSS, Sass, Hand-rolled Authentication, PostgreSQL database,  & Heroku.
•Fully optimized for mobile.
•Project completed in a 5 day sprint.

Show Me Your Junk

This app is a collaboration between myself and 2 classmates, designed with the idea of recycling household items in Los Angeles that would otherwise become part of the 12,000 tons of waste delivered to Puente Hills landfill each week. Puente Hills landfill is the largest rubbish dump in America.
This project was built in Node.js, Javascript, Express, and utilized MongoDB for the database. Views were rendered via EJS templates. I was responsible for Amazon s3 integration for the photo uploads, Google maps integration, Google GeoCoding integration, hand-rolled Passport authentication for the Facebook login, deployment to Heroku, managing the GitHub, as well as a good amount of the API build. The Facebook log in will not work unless I add you to a list of people who can use it (It's still in "Test" mode on the FB side), but you can create an account/posts/etc. We were the only group in our cohort to receive a perfect score on a project, and I'm very proud of that.
•Fully optimized for mobile.
•Project completed in a 5 day sprint.

Revisit

Revisit is a digital diary created by myself and another classmate. Users can upload photos and make "Entries" (single posts), or multiple photos to create "Chapters" (albums). We also added the option to upload a song for each Chapter, as music is so integral to certain memories or experiences that one has in their life.
This is a MEAN Stack app. I built the back-end API, and integrated Amazon s3 for photo and video uploads. I also managed the GitHub repo.
•Project completed in a 5 day sprint.
